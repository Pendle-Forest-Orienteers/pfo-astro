PFO is committed to providing a safe, welcoming environment for everyone who takes part in our events, including children, young people, and vulnerable adults.

## Our principles

- Everyone has the right to take part in orienteering free from harm, abuse or discrimination.
- The welfare of children and vulnerable adults is our paramount concern.
- All committee members and event organisers act in accordance with the British Orienteering Safeguarding Policy.
- Concerns are taken seriously, acted on promptly, and treated confidentially as far as legally possible.

## Welfare Officer

PFO's designated Welfare Officer is **Kay Hawke**. If you have a safeguarding concern relating to PFO, an event, a member, or a child or vulnerable adult connected with the club, please contact Kay directly via the [contacts page](/contacts).

In an emergency or where a child is at immediate risk, contact the police on 999. Subsequent reporting to PFO and British Orienteering should still happen so that the matter is properly recorded and managed.

## Junior participation

For all PFO events:

- Children aged 15 and under must be accompanied by a responsible adult, either running together as a pair or trio, or with the adult shadowing.
- Children aged 16–17 may compete on their own at a level appropriate to their experience, but must have written consent from a parent or guardian on file with British Orienteering and have provided emergency contact details.
- Photographs of juniors are taken only with parental consent and are not labelled with full names where possible.

## Training of officials

Committee members and regular event organisers complete the British Orienteering safeguarding awareness module. Volunteers who work directly with juniors hold an enhanced DBS check valid in the UK.

## Our wider commitments

PFO follows the [British Orienteering Safeguarding Policy](https://www.britishorienteering.org.uk/safeguarding) in full. Where club practice differs from BO guidance, BO guidance takes precedence.

This policy is reviewed annually by the committee.
