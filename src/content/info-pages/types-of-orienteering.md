Orienteering comes in a lot of flavours. Here's a quick guide to the formats you'll see at PFO events and what to expect from each.

## Forest

The classic. Held in woodland or moorland on a 1:10,000 or 1:15,000 scale orienteering map. You navigate between control points in a set order, on a course matched to your experience and age class. Courses range from "yellow" (very easy, for newcomers and children) to "black" (longest and most technical).

Typical event: 60–120 minutes' running time on the course.

## Urban

City- and town-centre orienteering. You're navigating through streets, ginnels, housing estates and parks at a faster pace. Map scale is usually 1:5,000 or 1:7,500.

Typical event: 30–60 minutes.

## Street-O

A relaxed, accessible cousin of urban orienteering. Usually a score event in the dark or twilight (winter midweek), you find as many controls as you can in a fixed time, often picking the order yourself. Walk, jog or run — beginners welcome.

Typical event: 45–75 minutes.

## Sprint

Short, fast urban or parkland racing. Sprint maps use a special 1:4,000 scale and a slightly different symbol set. Designed to be flat-out — courses are 12–25 minutes' winning time.

## Score

Visit as many controls as you can in a set time. Each control is worth points (sometimes equal, sometimes weighted by difficulty). Late finishers lose points per minute. Plan your route to maximise points before time's up.

## Night-O

Forest orienteering after dark. You'll need a head torch. Surprisingly fun — the map looks completely different at night and you really learn to trust the contour lines.

## MTBO

Mountain bike orienteering. You stay on rideable tracks and your map shows track width and quality, not the off-track detail. Course planning is more about choosing routes and managing energy than reading fine map detail.

## MapRun

Phone-app virtual orienteering — the app uses GPS to detect when you arrive at each control point. No physical posts on the ground. Run anywhere, any time, then upload your track to compare with others. Great for solo training or trying a new area.

## Anytime / Permanent Courses (POCs)

Permanent posts at fixed locations across our area. Pick up the map, run the course in your own time, no event entry needed. See the [Anytime Orienteering page](/information/anytime-orienteering) for the list of available POCs.
