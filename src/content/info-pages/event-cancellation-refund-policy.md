If a PFO event has to be cancelled, the following policy applies.

## When we cancel

PFO may cancel an event for any of the following reasons:

- Severe or unsafe weather
- Loss of venue access (landowner, council, or other permission withdrawn)
- Major incident in the area making the event unsafe
- Insufficient organising team available

The decision to cancel is made by the event controller in consultation with the organiser and committee chair. We will publish the decision on this website and on PFO's social media channels as early as possible.

## Refunds

If we cancel before the published entry close date, all online entry fees are refunded in full via SI Entries within 14 days. There is no need to request the refund — it happens automatically.

If we cancel after the entry close date but before the event takes place, refunds are issued in full where possible, less any non-recoverable costs (printed maps, hired equipment, venue deposits) which may be deducted at the committee's discretion.

If an event is part-completed and then cancelled (for example, abandoned during severe weather), no refund is due, but results may not be official.

## Personal cancellations

If you have to withdraw from an event after entering, contact the organiser as soon as possible. Refunds are at the organiser's discretion and depend on whether the event is fully subscribed.

## Force majeure

In exceptional circumstances (national lockdown, infectious disease guidance, weather emergency declared by authorities), the committee reserves the right to vary this policy on a case-by-case basis, with the priority being safety and fairness to the membership.
