PFO is a member club of British Orienteering. This means our events are covered by British Orienteering's public liability insurance, with two important things to know.

## What's covered

The British Orienteering insurance covers:

- Public liability for the club, event organisers, and members during PFO-organised events
- Member-on-member incidents at events
- Accidents to members of the public caused by the event taking place

## What changed in January 2026

From 1 January 2026, **participants who are not members of British Orienteering are no longer covered** by British Orienteering's public liability insurance.

What this means in practice:

- If you are a BO member, you are covered as before — no action needed.
- If you are not a BO member and choose to take part in a PFO event, you do so at your own risk. You would not be covered if you accidentally injured another member of the public during the event or damaged property.
- This is consistent across all British Orienteering clubs nationally — it isn't a PFO-specific change.

If you orienteer regularly, joining the club is the simplest fix. Membership covers PFO, NWOA and British Orienteering as a single annual fee — see our [membership page](/membership).

## Personal injury

Public liability insurance protects you from claims by *others*. It does not cover *your own* injuries. If you'd like cover for personal injury — particularly if you also race competitively — your home contents insurance or a sports-specific personal accident policy is worth looking at.
